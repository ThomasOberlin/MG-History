2016 ‚Äî Origin & founding

South America's first commercial CEA farm

Urban Farm Modern Horticulture & Technology Ltd., trading as MightyGreens, was founded in 2016 in Rio de Janeiro as the first controlled environment agriculture (CEA) company operating commercially in South America.

The seed of the business was planted when two of the founders spent over a year witnessing a friend's daily struggle in Venezuela to access food and water ‚Äî driving them to seek solutions for local production in controlled environments.

2016 ‚Äì 2019 ‚Äî First generation

Microgreens at scale

Between 2016 and 2019, MightyGreens developed a pioneering large-scale microgreens project, integrating cutting-edge agronomy, greenhouse technology, and CEA systems.

The initial units, operating in compact spaces, demonstrated that it was possible to grow ultra-young, nutrient-dense microgreens with significantly reduced water usage and zero pesticides in an urban environment.

2018 ‚Äì 2019 ‚Äî Modular expansion

Vertical farms & container units

The company evolved into modular vertical farms built inside shipping containers ‚Äî true "plant data centers" ‚Äî with precise control over light, temperature, humidity, and ventilation.

This model made it possible to install farms in dense urban areas and next to supermarkets, shortening the logistics chain and democratising access to fresh produce year-round.

Investment rounds & accelerated growth

Between 2022 and 2023, MightyGreens received two investment rounds from Raio Capital totalling approximately R$ 2.32 million, earmarked to structure and consolidate CEA microgreens and mushroom operations.

This enabled the company to accelerate capacity expansion, complete onboarding with major retail chains, and develop new modular farm units for clients across Brazil.

2024 onwards

CEA technology platform

Today, MightyGreens is recognised as a controlled environment agriculture platform, combining own production with technology, services, and training for urban farms and partner producers.

Recent interviews with leadership reinforce the vision of scaling vertical mushroom and microgreens farm models to different regions of Brazil, maintaining a focus on sustainability and operational efficiency.
