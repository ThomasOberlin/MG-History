2016 ‚Äî Foundation

South America's first commercial CEA farm ¬∑ Angel round closed

Urban Farm Modern Horticulture & Technology Ltd., trading as MightyGreens, was founded in Rio de Janeiro as the first controlled environment agriculture (CEA) company operating commercially in South America. The idea was born after two of the founders spent over a year witnessing a close friend's daily struggle in Venezuela to access food and clean water ‚Äî a firsthand lesson that drove them toward local, controlled-environment food production as a genuine solution.

To get off the ground, the team raised an initial angel investment from friends and family, providing the seed capital needed to design the first growing systems and begin commercial operations.

2017 ‚Äî First revenue ¬∑ First invention

Live microgreens reach restaurant tables ¬∑ Fogponics system deployed ¬∑ Second angel round

MightyGreens began selling live microgreens directly to restaurants in Rio de Janeiro ‚Äî the company's first commercial revenue stream and an early proof point that chefs valued the superior freshness of a product delivered still alive. Feedback from this channel shaped the team's understanding of quality standards and handling requirements at scale.

That same year, the engineering team designed, built, and deployed a proprietary fogponics germination system ‚Äî using ultra-fine water microdroplets to maintain optimal humidity and moisture at root level with minimal water and energy input. The system dramatically accelerated germination rates and became the foundation for later innovations. A second angel investment round was closed, providing capital to expand production capacity and continue R&D.

2018 ‚Äî Retail entry ¬∑ Hardware innovation

Supermarket launch in Rio ¬∑ Mechanical microgreens seeder designed & tested

MightyGreens moved beyond restaurants and launched live microgreens sales in Rio de Janeiro supermarkets ‚Äî a significantly more demanding channel requiring consistent volume, uniform presentation, and reliable shelf life. Retail partners included Zona Sul, HortiFruti, and Farina Pura. The team successfully met the rigorous requirements of these chains, establishing the company as a credible retail supplier.

In parallel, the engineering team designed, built, and tested a mechanical microgreens seeder ‚Äî a custom automation tool that standardised seed dosage and tray distribution, slashing the time and labour cost of the seeding step and making industrial-scale production economically viable.

2019 ‚Äî National expansion ¬∑ Two breakthrough innovations

S√£o Paulo retail launch ¬∑ 50+ restaurants served ¬∑ Waterless substrate ¬∑ On-site mushroom container deployed

MightyGreens expanded its live microgreens retail presence to S√£o Paulo supermarkets ‚Äî Brazil's largest consumer market ‚Äî adding St Marche and Santa Maria to its network alongside continued growth with Rio partners Zona Sul, HortiFruti, Farina Pura, La Frutaria, Organo Mix, and Deli Del√≠cia. By this point, the company was also supplying more than 50 restaurants, cementing its position as the leading live microgreens supplier in Brazil's premium food channel.

Two landmark proprietary innovations were delivered that year. First, the team developed and deployed a waterless edible vegan gel substrate for microgreens, eliminating the need for hydroponic pumping systems entirely and dramatically reducing both water consumption and energy costs. Second, MightyGreens deployed an on-site mushroom production container at a supermarket in Rio de Janeiro ‚Äî a self-contained unit engineered to capture 100% of its own water through a closed-loop harvesting system, placing live production metres from the supermarket shelf.

2020 ‚Äî Pandemic ¬∑ Circular economy pivot

Microgreens production halted ¬∑ Circular economy mushroom substrate created

The COVID-19 pandemic forced MightyGreens to halt microgreens production as foodservice and retail supply chains collapsed. Rather than standing still, the team redirected its energy into solving a different problem: substrate supply and waste.

The result was a circular economy mushroom substrate model ‚Äî a system that converted organic waste generated by retail partners into nutrient-rich growing substrate for mushroom production. By closing the loop between retail waste and new food output, the model cut substrate costs and embedded MightyGreens more deeply into the supply chains of its supermarket partners.

MightyGreens was recapitalised through an investment from venture capital firm Raio Capital, enabling the company to rebuild and significantly upgrade its operations. The facility incorporated a suite of advanced technologies: an AI voice-controlled production management system, automatic fogponics delivering ultra-fine water microdroplets for precise humidity and climate control, and targeted lighting wavelengths matched to the optimal growth spectrum for each mushroom variety. With the facility operational, MightyGreens returned to selling Asian mushrooms ‚Äî primarily shiitake ‚Äî to supermarket chains including Zona Sul and HortiFruti across Rio de Janeiro.

2022 ‚Äî Second Raio investment ¬∑ Sustainability milestone

Follow-on VC round ¬∑ Working capital for substrate ¬∑ 100% compostable packaging launched

Raio Capital made a follow-on investment, bringing total funding from the firm to approximately R$ 2.32 million. With mushroom sales growing rapidly across retail partners including Zona Sul, HortiFruti, Farina Pura, La Frutaria, Organo Mix, and Deli Del√≠cia in Rio and St Marche and Santa Maria in S√£o Paulo, the company was caught in a cash flow squeeze: substrate required 90-day lead time purchasing, while supermarket payment cycles ran to 45 days. That same year, MightyGreens launched 100% compostable mushroom packaging made from cellulose film and sugarcane bagasse trays. Internal testing showed the format significantly extended shelf life, reduced post-harvest losses at the supermarket level, and maintained sensory quality throughout the cold chain ‚Äî while eliminating single-use plastic from the product line entirely.

2023 ‚Äî Product diversification trials

With a stable production base and established retail relationships, MightyGreens ran a series of product trials ‚Äî none of which were commercialised ‚Äî exploring value-added mushroom formats. MightyGreens shifted focus from direct production to project delivery ‚Äî selling and installing turnkey microgreens and mushroom production systems for local producers across Brazil. Following this strategic shift, the S√£o Crist√≥v√£o mushroom production facility was shut down, completing the transition from a vertically integrated producer to a technology and consulting platform.

2025 ‚Äî Distribution pivot ¬∑ Platform expansion

MightyGreens pivoted to distributing Asian mushrooms sourced from partner producers to supermarket chains ‚Äî maintaining retail market presence without the capital intensity of own production. This asset-light distribution model allows the company to serve established retail clients while keeping fixed costs minimal.

On the technology platform side, MightyGreens significantly expanded its CEA project offering beyond mushrooms and microgreens to include hops, fodder, basil, and medical marijuana ‚Äî applying the same controlled-environment expertise, proprietary hardware, and growing protocols across a diversified portfolio of high-value crops.
